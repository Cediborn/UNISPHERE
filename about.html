About ClashBoard

A small tool for a common first-semester problem.

Every semester, University of Ghana students are assigned to course groups scattered across different lecture halls, and the timetable never looks the same twice. Working out whether Group 3 of one course collides with Group GK-D of another usually means lining up several PDFs by hand.

ClashBoard turns that into a form: add each class once, and any overlapping time slot is flagged immediately — on the weekly board and through a quick "would this clash?" check for anything you're thinking of adding, like a study group or a part-time shift.

How it works

Everything runs in your browser. There's no server, no account, and no submission button that sends your timetable anywhere. Your schedule is saved to your device using local browser storage so it's there when you come back — see the privacy page for the full detail.

Who it's for

Built with Level 100 students in mind, but the day/time/venue format works for any course, group, or level. The course-code suggestions are drawn from UG's published Level 100 catalog, but you can type any code.

Feedback

This is an early, open version. If a feature would help — clash checks across a full week of hypothetical additions, exporting the board as an image, or dark/light themes — open an issue on the project's GitHub repository.
